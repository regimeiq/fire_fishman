# Findings Summary

| Area | Finding | Evidence | Caveat |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Prospect development | Velocity- and pitch-specific whiff metrics were more informative than aggregate discipline metrics. | Whiff rate vs 96+ mph velocity created the clearest separation by outcome; cohort-level chase-rate separation was small, with the chase collapse concentrated in the Yankees case studies. | Small prospect cohort; labels can change over time. |
| Ben Rice | Rice was the strongest internal validation case. | Passed 5/5 readiness gates; Volpe and Dominguez each passed 4/5, and Peraza passed 3/5. Rice then broke out in 2025 and had a strong early-2026 public-data sample. | 2026 is ongoing and should be updated after the season. |
| Dominguez and Volpe | Both remain live evaluations. | Dominguez's injury interrupts the signal; Volpe's swing-plane adjustment needs more sample. | Do not treat either case as closed. |
| Organization comparison | Yankees outcomes lagged top comparison orgs in the curated cohort. | NYY 2/6 successful outcomes versus BAL 5/5 and CLE 3/5 among target orgs. | Organization samples are modest. |
| Baserunning | Yankees baserunning value declined from a prior strength. | BsR moved from +7.6 in 2017 to -17.2 in 2024; 2018-2024 cumulative BsR was -39.2. | BsR is a run-value estimate, not a full accounting of every estimated cost. |
| Defense | Defensive underperformance was not permanent. | Weak 2018-2021 public defensive metrics were followed by a sharp 2022 improvement. | Public fielding metrics have noise and can differ by source. |
| Roster construction | Several rosters leaned toward extreme hitter profiles. | Case studies compare all-or-nothing power and low-impact contact profiles against contender archetypes. | Archetype thresholds are descriptive. |
| Team value composite | Pressure, baserunning, and defense added descriptive signal beyond offense. | Same-year r = +0.34 with WAR; year-ahead r = +0.24. | Exploratory metric; not causal. |
